Positioning
My point of view

Change fails when it's treated as a comms problem. I work from the other end — starting with who has to change, why they won't, and what it would take to earn their commitment — then building the governance, training, and measurement infrastructure to make adoption visible and sustainable. I'm most useful in environments where the workforce is technically minded, site cultures are distinct, and there's no established playbook. I've run end-to-end change programs in mining, manufacturing, and the care sector; led post-merger integrations; and built executive dashboards that surface risk before it becomes a delivery problem. I don't treat frameworks as finished products. I work inside whatever structure exists, identify where it breaks under real program conditions, and bring evidence to improve it.

Change Management · Risk Visibility
Training & Compliance Dashboard — making risk visible before it becomes a problem
Built an executive-facing Power BI dashboard to surface training compliance risk signals across a multi-site manufacturing operation.
GFG Alliance (OneSteel Manufacturing)
Power BIChange governanceMulti-site
ChallengeTraining compliance was tracked in spreadsheets per site, with no consolidated view for leadership. Risk signals — lapsed certifications, safety-critical gaps — were invisible until they became incidents or audit findings.
My roleEnd-to-end ownership: requirements gathering across site managers, dashboard design and build in Power BI, stakeholder onboarding, and ongoing iteration based on leadership feedback.
ApproachIdentified the decisions leadership needed to make, not just the data they had. Designed the dashboard around those decisions — flagging at-risk sites and workforce segments rather than displaying raw completion rates. Built in drill-down so site managers could act without waiting for a report cycle.
OutcomeLeadership shifted from reactive to proactive on training compliance. Compliance conversations moved from monthly paper packs to weekly dashboard reviews. Risk flagging now happens before certification lapses, not after.
ReflectionI'd spend more time earlier on defining what "at risk" meant to each stakeholder — the first version tracked the wrong leading indicators for two of the four sites.

Instructional Design · eLearning
Agile 101 for a mining workforce — translating abstract methodology for people who build real things
Designed and developed a video-based eLearning program introducing Agile principles to a BHP mining workforce with no prior exposure to the methodology.
BHP
eLearningVideo productionAgile
ChallengeThe organisation was adopting Agile ways of working, but the workforce — highly technical, practically oriented, and sceptical of frameworks — needed more than a theory overview. Generic off-the-shelf Agile content felt irrelevant to people who measure their work in tonnes, shifts, and safety records.
My roleDesign and development of the full eLearning series — scripting, storyboarding, video production, and implementation. Worked closely with SMEs to ground Agile concepts in mining-specific scenarios.
ApproachLed with the problem Agile solves, not the framework itself. Used operational analogies familiar to the workforce — shift handovers as stand-ups, maintenance sprints — to make abstract concepts concrete before introducing terminology. Videos kept short (under 5 minutes) to fit shift-based access patterns.
OutcomeProgram deployed across target cohorts with strong completion rates. Qualitative feedback indicated the scenarios resonated; learners connected the methodology to work they already did rather than seeing it as external imposition.
ReflectionI'd build in a short "So what does this mean for your team?" prompt at the end of each module — the learning landed better in teams where managers debriefed than where it was completed alone.

03
Change Management · Platform Adoption
Workplace by Meta rollout at Cara — embedding a comms platform into daily operations
Led the end-to-end change program to adopt Workplace as the organisation's centralised communications platform, moving from fragmented channels to a single source of connection.
Cara
Platform adoptionChange commsStakeholder management
ChallengeThe organisation lacked a unified internal comms channel. Information was fragmented across email, noticeboards, and informal networks — with frontline workers often the last to receive updates. Leadership wanted a platform solution; the change program had to make people want to use it.
My roleChange program lead — stakeholder mapping, comms plan, training design, champion network, adoption tracking, and post-launch embedding. Not just the technical rollout.
ApproachIdentified early adopters in each team and gave them a role before the launch — they became the proof that the platform was useful, not a mandate. Segmented communications by workforce type. Tracked active usage weekly, not just account creation, and intervened with teams showing low engagement.
OutcomePlatform moved from a tool IT owned to one staff used by choice. Internal communications shifted measurably from email to Workplace for operational updates within the first quarter post-launch.
ReflectionI underestimated how much the annual report video (produced as part of the same initiative) would serve as a trust signal — people engaged more with the platform once they saw the organisation communicating transparently through it.
